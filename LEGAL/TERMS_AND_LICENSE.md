# Terms and end-user license decision

## App Store end-user license

Use Apple’s standard Licensed Application End User License Agreement for the first release:

<https://www.apple.com/legal/internet-services/itunes/dev/stdeula/>

Apple applies this standard agreement automatically when no custom EULA is entered in App Store Connect. A custom EULA is therefore not required for the current free, offline-first app.

Do not paste this repository document into App Store Connect as a custom EULA. If subscriptions, paid services, accounts, user-generated content, or a publisher-operated backend are added later, obtain legal review before deciding whether custom terms are appropriate.

## In-app educational disclaimer

The app is an educational aid. Translations, definitions, examples, speech transcripts, and review schedules may contain errors and are not professional, legal, medical, or safety advice. This wording is presented in the app’s About & Privacy screen and supplements rather than replaces Apple’s standard EULA.

## Required user acceptance

The current app does not need an extra first-launch checkbox or click-through agreement. There are no accounts, purchases, subscriptions, user uploads, or publisher-operated services that require separate contractual consent. Adding such a gate without a concrete legal need would add friction and would not replace App Store consent to Apple’s EULA.
