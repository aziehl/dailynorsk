# Privacy Policy for Daily Norsk

Effective date: 16 July 2026

The publisher and data controller is the developer or seller identified on the app’s App Store product page. Privacy questions can be sent through the current Support link on that product page.

## Summary

Daily Norsk has no accounts, advertising, analytics, tracking, cloud sync, or publisher-operated server. The publisher does not receive learning history, library searches, speech transcripts, audio recordings, or device identifiers from the app.

## Information stored on the device

Learning progress, review scheduling, and pronunciation-attempt counts are stored locally using Apple’s SwiftData framework. Widget selection and rotation state are stored in the app’s private App Group container using `UserDefaults`. This information is used only to provide the app’s learning, review, progress, and widget features.

The user can erase learning progress from the Progress screen. Deleting the app removes its local app data, subject to normal device and backup behavior controlled by Apple and the user.

## Microphone and speech recognition

Speech practice is optional. It begins only after the user taps the recognition control and grants microphone and speech-recognition permission.

The app does not save audio recordings. When the installed Norwegian recognizer supports on-device processing, the app requires recognition to remain on the device. Otherwise, audio is sent to Apple’s speech-recognition service to service the immediate recognition request. The publisher does not receive the audio or resulting transcript. Apple’s processing is governed by Apple’s privacy policy: <https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/>.

The transcript exists in memory only while the practice interface is in use. The app stores only a local count that a practice attempt occurred; it does not store the transcript or recognition result.

Microphone and speech-recognition permission can be revoked at any time in iOS Settings. Speech practice is optional and the rest of the app remains usable without it.

## Data collection, sharing, and tracking

The app does not collect personal data as Apple defines collection for App Store privacy disclosures. It does not sell or share personal data, use data for advertising, or track users across apps or websites. The app includes no third-party analytics, advertising, or social SDKs.

The app opens external legal or acknowledgement links in the system browser only when the user selects them. The destination website’s operator is responsible for its own data practices.

## Retention and deletion

The publisher has no user account or publisher-held personal data to retain or delete. Locally stored progress remains until the user resets it, deletes the app, or restores/replaces device data through Apple’s device and backup features.

## Children

The app is a general-audience educational tool and does not knowingly collect personal information from children or any other users. It should not be marked “Made for Kids” in App Store Connect unless the publisher intentionally adopts all requirements for that category.

## Changes

If the app’s data practices change, this policy and the App Store privacy answers must be updated before the changed version is released. The effective date above identifies the current policy version.

## Publishing requirement

This document must be hosted at a stable, publicly accessible HTTPS URL controlled by the publisher. Enter that URL in App Store Connect’s Privacy Policy field and keep the same policy accessible from inside the app.
