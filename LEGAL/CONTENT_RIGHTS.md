# Content rights release record

This record supports the Content Rights declaration in App Store Connect. It is an engineering and provenance record, not a substitute for advice from the publisher’s lawyer.

## Current distributed app

- The curated Vestland definitions, translations, usage notes, regional-form notes, examples, and phrases were written specifically for this project.
- The expanded frequency lexicon adapts English glosses and inflection evidence from English Wiktionary through the checksum-pinned Kaikki.org extraction identified in `CONTENT_SOURCES.md`.
- Wiktionary-derived lexical fields and Daily Norsk’s adaptations of those fields are distributed under CC BY-SA 4.0 with source, contributor-history, license, and modification notices in-app and in `THIRD_PARTY_NOTICES.md`.
- Most generated Norwegian–English example pairs are selected from the checksum-pinned Tatoeba export identified in `CONTENT_SOURCES.md`. Sentence IDs and contributor names are retained in the release evidence; the selected text is distributed under CC BY 2.0 France with attribution and modification notices in-app and in `THIRD_PARTY_NOTICES.md`.
- The two-line `Daily` / `norsk` app icon is project-authored artwork rendered with Avenir Next Demi Bold into the asset catalog.
- The app contains no third-party SDK or package dependencies.
- Apple system frameworks are used under the Apple Developer Program terms.

For this scope, the App Store Connect Content Rights answer should disclose that the app contains licensed third-party dictionary content and that the publisher has the required rights under CC BY-SA 4.0. The publisher must preserve attribution and ShareAlike compliance and confirm ownership of the remaining project-authored material before certifying the answer.

## Editorial evidence excluded from the binary

`wordfreq` and Universal Dependencies Norwegian Bokmål remain pinned source references for offline candidate generation. Their repositories, full datasets, candidate reports, and corpus sentences are not part of the app or widget target. The promoted Wiktionary-derived lexical fields and Tatoeba example pairs are the explicit exceptions and carry their required notices. See `THIRD_PARTY_NOTICES.md` and `CONTENT_SOURCES.md`.

Before each generated-content release, preserve the evidence report, source checksum, license, attribution, and modification notice. Record fluent-speaker review separately from licensing review; an open license permits redistribution but does not guarantee linguistic accuracy.
